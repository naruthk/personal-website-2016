Paradise Lost:

Satan's March to Glory

This illustrated adaptation tackles John Milton's classic literary work, Paradise Lost, and brings to life the story mostly through the perspective of Satan, the fallen angel. All is lost for Satan after he has been kicked out of heaven.

My adaptation will be loosely based on John Milton's original work.

Notable Characters

God

Regarded as the Supreme Commander, he reigns over all of heaven with love and joy. He's also the creator of Adam and Eve. One can only view his spiritual body against the clouds.

Son

The Father's only Son. His kindness and eternal love for mankind makes him one of the most important characters besides Satan. God also credits him for creating the universe.

Satan

Also known as Lucifer. He is once a beloved angel in heaven who later spread evil into the world of mankind. He hates God, partly because of how he's always the controller of everything.

Adam

He's the husband of Eve, and he loves his wife dearly. Both Adam and Eve were created by God, and Adam takes care of his beloved wife, Eve, as they live happily in the Garden of Eden.

Eve

The wife of Adam, and one who was tricked to eat the forbidden fruit by Satan who disguised himself as a serpent. Some would consider Eve's bad actions to be the leading cause of sin.

Fallen Friends

Satan and his friends, compelled by their evil nature to dethrone and end being servants of God, were kicked out of Heaven with force and power. To him and his friends, falling into Hell seemed like a dragging period of eternity. "Nine times the Space that measures Day and Night," (Book I, 50)... that was how long the period lasted.

Of course, when in Hell, their bodies do not burn, for they are immortals after all. But their pride and ego, specially for Satan, burned hotter than the fiery fires in the foreign land they now stood on.

But Satan wondered. Why abandoned them into distant land but not destroyed them? Why set them to be stuck in hell forever for a crime they think they are not deserving to be blamed? Why is God in control of everything? The questions unanswered.

Strangers in A World of No Light

Like a group of wild deers, adventurous in traits but foolish enough to try and explore unknown territories marked by dangerous predators, the banned angels of Heaven toured the visited terrains. The expansive and horrifying lake of fire, mixed with the unidentified terrains in Hell, made the involuntary visitors felt unwelcoming. They did not realize the unexpected - the climatic fall they did not ask for. A request one soul did not ask for nor wish. Their visit to a kingdom that neither mankind nor the most evil of the beasts on earth had ever stepped foot on before.

Flames forever lid. "Yet from those flames, no light but rather darkness visible..." (Book I, 62-3). Searching for hope? Be lost... for "hope never comes that comes to all" (Book I, 66-7). Such a tormented region of sorrow and darkness hell was.

Satan questioned God's creation of Hell, its existence and purpose. He also continued wondering about the same unanswered questions. The longer the questions were unanswered, the more powerful Satan's evil nature enlarged.

"The mind is its own place, and in itself can make a heaven of hell, a hell of heaven."

Book I, 254-5

Condemned to hell to remain there for eternity. Casted away to the land of despair. Such fate unchanging. What magnificent potent could heal this maleficent soul?

"What better way than to treat Hell as great as Heaven?," said Satan. "Let's reign in Hell. It's better than being having no freedom and serving in Heaven."

That was Satan's way of earning nothing more than hope and a fraction of happiness. Nothing more than a forgotten angel, seeking freedom, breaking free.

Satan asks, "What should I do?"

There's never a soul so evil he can no longer chase after the light. But Satan asked, "If you were me, will you revenge God, or view the beauty of Heaven's Garden of Eden and be nice so that one day God will come to me and take me back with him to Paradise?"

The Plan to Revenge God

Armed not with weaponry but with a dangerous and vicious mind, the castaway party devised a plan to wreck heaven, to end what is forever glorious, to dethrone the Father of Creation, for he is a leader that forces you to obey him or be punished. Satan had all of this in mind and he positioned himself as the commander of the fallen army, and met with praises and acceptance by his devil army that surrounded him.

"String laws imposed, to celebrate his throne with warbled hymns, and to his godhead sing forced hallelujahs; while he lordly sits our envied sovereign...", the evil commander spoke with anger (Book II, 241-4).

And through his speech the supporters added fuel to the burning flames.

Who would it be, daring and bold, to get out of this inferno? Satan stepped up. Fear was not his trait. He journeyed through vast regions of Hell, made it out alive, and soared up to Heaven, only this time as a forbidden visitor named Satan.

Heaven & God's Garden

The Father and His Son lived here. Satan saw it with his own eyes: the former in the form of lights visible through clouds. The angels too, though only those who did not rebel against him. Satan was not allowed to enter this beautiful kingdom. The evilness in him all too evil, followed by his inert desire to break free from God, to stop serving the Father... all these were the accumulation of sin Satan had collected. And because of this, because of who he was and who he wanted to be, prevented him from freely entering the Garden of Eden ever again.

He was stopped by Gabriel, guardian of the garden.

Gabriel spoke, "Satan, I know your strength, and you know mine. Neither is our own but given. So why boast what our powers can do when the Father's power is inevitably much more powerful than ours?" He then added, "Look up to the light. See for yourself how weak you are if you resist." (Book IV, 1005-13)

Rage Builds Up as the Son is Anointed

"This day I have begot whom I declare my only son...," spoke God. "You all must behold him at my right hand and all in heaven must bow. (Book V, 603-8)

Satan now had two individuals that he had to "serve". He hated this fact the most. He envied the Son of God. As everybody all around praised and rejoiced in happiness, only Satan watched with grief and regret. He lost God's favor.

"...How provident he is, how far from thought to make us less...," Satan spoke. (Book V, lines 828-9).

He could not keep his evil nature from imploding out. He wanted revenge, no more remaining quiet and calm. Eventually, he successfully persuaded some of the other angels to be on his side and form an army against God.

Let the War Begin

God spoke, "Lead forth to battle my armed saints by thousands and by millions ranged for fight; equal in number to that godless crew rebellious (Book VI, Lines 46-50).

The war began, and surely enough an epic thrilling battle was occuring, with "flaming arms and chariots" (Book 6, 17).

The war was fought, with other angles on God's side first against Satan's army.

  • Next

Then it became time for Gabriel and Michael, God's beloved angels, and their showdown against the enemy.

  • Next

Satan Bleeds

In what could be referred to as the final showdown among the angels, Michael's sword swiftly cut through all of Satan's right side.

That's when "Satan first knew pain" (Book VI, 327). But worst than pain was pride. His pride was shattered. He was "shame to find himself not matchless" (Book VI, 341).

  • Next

"None but you can end it"

War continued to shatter Heaven's grounds. God, the creator of all things in heaven and earth, saw it displeasing and sad to see the evil that Satan had brought upon heaven and the destruction that he had caused.

"For you I have ordained it, and so far have suffered, that the glory may be your of ending this great war, since none but you can end it." So God spoke to His Son.

Hearing his Father's words, the Son marched at speed into the battle field, shooting arrows against an army of rebellious angels.

Fallen Friends

The Son grabbed the rebellious angels as if they were herd of goats. Frightened and amazed by the power of the Son, they had no choice but to escape down a "spacious gap disclosed into the wasteful deep" (Book VI, lines 859-62).

The rebellious and frightened angels made the jump.

Thus, Satan and his friends, were kicked out of Heaven with force and power. "Nine times the Space that measures Day and Night," (Book I, 50)... that was how long the period lasted.

  • Next

War Was Over

Satan values the importance of having free will. For him he desires the ability to be in control of what he wants to do. He does not hate Heaven; in fact, in some occasions he actually really thinks the Heaven is so pure and beautiful by nature. He just wants others to recognize the pain he is going through.

The story can end here, if you so wanted it. The remainder of the story is a fairly simple but famous plot concerning Eve and the evil serpent as the former is tempted to eat the forbidden fruit.

You (as the reader) have the choice to make, like Satan has.

Satan Now A Serpent

The fallen angel, lost but not forgetten, came back and somehow went through the gates that protect the Garden of Eden. One wonders how he does so. But nevertheless, the rebellious angel found the perfect animal to disguise himself as.

  • Next

Eve Confronts the Serpent

When morning came, Satan slowly navigated his way through paradise and finally saw Eve, pure and beautiful as she was.

Satan watched with deceitful eyes the woman that stood before him. He slithered his way near her, and atop the forbidden tree he rested; his body coiled in circle.

Satan to Eve: "To satisfy the sharp desire I had of tasting those fair apples, I resolved not to defer because I was hungry and thirsty. The alluring fruit was hard to deny." (Book IX, 585-8).

Satan tempted Eve to the point of perfection. He boldly proclaimed that he (as a snake) gained the power of wisdom and speech because of that fruit.

  • Next

But the poor girl, struck by the serpent's ability to talk to her, was tempted. The serpent's confidence grew larger. He convinced her to eat the fruit from the forbidden tree - a direct violation of God's command.

But Eve first showed sign of unwillingness.

"But of this tree we may not taste nor touch; god so commanded" (Book IX, 651-2).

Irresistible yet so inviting, how terrible the outcome would be to offend the Father. The choice so many and yet this particular fruit, bright and alluring, delicious it must be.

  • Next

"She plucked, she ate"

After all, it was hard to defer a chance to take a bite of that delicious-looking fruit. And she did so. Eve at the apple. That was all it take for mankind to sin. Short, quick, a swift act. An unforgivable crime had happened.

She ate first, and later Adam. "Earth felt the wound" (Book IX, 782). But why no warning? Why did the Father, he who is loving and possesses immense power to see even beyond the current pace in time, not warn the victims of the serpent's crime?

  • Next

Punishment

So the rebellions, with Satan in particular, deserving of their crime, were punished. Satan involuntarily was turned into a serpent, along with his other friends in hell.

Adam and Eve would not live forever, but they earned a chance of bearing another child so that newer generations can continue.

The original Paradise Lost (not this adaptation) mentioned that Adam thought God's decision to punish him and Eve was justifiable, but he still wondered why God would have let all the chaos happened especially because God was in control of everything. Perhaps, Satan thought about these concerning questions about fate and free will too. After all, all decisions were made by God ever since the beginning.

Leaving the Garden of Eden

The couple left the land of paradise, with God telling Adam enough of what will happen in the future, but not enough to let Adam and Eve remain in the Garden.

The gate to the Garden was locked forever. Too sinful man was, not worthy of entering the Holy place of God.
